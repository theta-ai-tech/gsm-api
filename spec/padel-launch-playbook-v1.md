# GSM — Padel-First Launch Playbook

*Athens beachhead · Club seeding · Founding league · Content calendar · MVP tweaks*

## 1. Product Vision: Padel-First Positioning

GSM is the scoring standard for amateur racket sports. We are launching as a padel-first app because padel is where the need is sharpest, the community is most socially active, and no scoring standard exists yet.

| The Updated Pitch |
| :---- |
| GSM is the first rating system for amateur padel. Find opponents at your level, play matches, log scores, and build a competitive identity that follows you across courts and cities. Tennis and pickleball support is built-in from day one — but padel is home. |

### 1.1 Why Padel First

* **No amateur scoring standard exists for padel.** Tennis has UTR and WTN (institutionally gated). Padel has nothing. GSM fills a genuine vacuum.

* **The community is socially native.** Padel players already organise through Instagram and WhatsApp. They share court photos, tag opponents, post results informally. GSM gives them a structured, shareable format for what they already do.

* **Higher density per venue.** Padel is doubles-heavy and courts are clustered in newer facilities. A single padel club has a tighter, more interconnected community than a traditional tennis club. Word of mouth travels faster.

* **Playtomic's gap.** Playtomic dominates court booking but their player intelligence layer is thin. In padel specifically, there is no competitor doing scoring, scouting, or rivalry tracking.

### 1.2 Multi-Sport by Design, Padel by Default

The app remains a multi-sport platform technically. During onboarding, users select their sport(s) and the architecture handles per-sport rankings, streaks, and Skill DNA independently. But every default, every marketing asset, and every early partnership leads with padel.

* Onboarding defaults to padel as the pre-selected sport
* Discovery feed prioritises padel players in the user's area
* Level descriptions use padel-specific language and context
* Tennis and pickleball are one tap away but not in the user's face on day one

## 3. The Founding League

The founding league is not just a feature test. It is your primary user acquisition and retention mechanism for the first 8 weeks. It gives people a concrete reason to download the app, log matches, and keep coming back.

### 3.1 Format

| Parameter | Decision |
| :---- | :---- |
| **Name** | GSM Athens Padel League — Season Zero |
| **Sport** | Padel (doubles) |
| **Format** | Round-robin groups of 6-8 players. Each player must complete at least 4 matches in 4 weeks. |
| **Duration** | 4 weeks (shorter than the standard 8-week league to create urgency and faster feedback loops) |
| **Entry fee** | Free for Season Zero. This is a beta test and a marketing tool, not a revenue event. |
| **Group seeding** | Self-declared level at signup. Groups formed by tier (Amateur, Intermediate, Advanced). Mixed levels within a group are fine for Season Zero — the scoring system handles point asymmetry. |
| **Match organisation** | Self-organised via the app. Players use the PLAY tab to find and challenge their group opponents. No fixed schedule. |
| **Score logging** | Both players verify through GSM. All matches count towards GSM score. |
| **Prizes** | Group winners: branded GSM merchandise (cap, wristband) + "Founding Champion" badge on their Athlete Card permanently. Overall league winner: featured on GSM Instagram + trophy. |
| **Target size** | 24-32 players across 4 groups. Enough for critical mass; small enough to manage manually. |
| **Host venue** | Co-branded with Ten Twenty Club or Flisvos Padel Academy. Final day (if feasible) is a mini-event at the venue. |

### 3.2 Why This Format Works

* **Mandatory minimum matches** forces engagement. A player who signs up but never plays is a wasted seat. 4 matches in 4 weeks is achievable but requires using the app repeatedly.

* **Self-organisation through the app** validates the core matchmaking loop. If players can't find and schedule their group matches through GSM, that's critical feedback.

* **Free entry removes all barriers.** Season One (Month 4) introduces a paid entry fee once the format is validated and demand is proven.

* **The "Founding Champion" badge** is a permanent status symbol. It creates FOMO for anyone who joins after Season Zero. "You should have been here from the start."

### 3.3 League Timeline

| When | Action | Channel |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Week 5** | Announce the founding league. Open registration (Google Form initially, in-app later). | Instagram, club WhatsApp groups, in-app banner |
| **Week 6** | Close registration at 32 players. Publish groups. Ensure all players have downloaded the app. | Direct message to each registrant |
| **Week 7-10** | League matches in progress. Weekly leaderboard update posted to Instagram. Mid-league check-in with players who haven't logged a match. | App + Instagram Stories |
| **Week 11** | League concludes. Winners announced. Badges assigned. Instagram feature post for the champion. Announce Season One registration opening. | Instagram + in-app + club partnership |

| Platform | Purpose | Frequency |

| **TikTok** | Short-form clips: courtside score logging, victory celebrations, "how GSM works" demos. | 2-3x/week |
| **WhatsApp/Telegram** | Direct community channel. League updates, feature announcements, beta feedback. | 2x/week max (avoid spam) |
| **X (Twitter)** | Product updates, padel news commentary, engagement with padel community accounts. | As needed |

### 4.2 Content Pillars

**Pillar 1: Match Cards.** Auto-generated victory cards from the app showing score, player names, GSM rating, and branding. These are designed to be screenshotted and shared to Stories. This is the core organic growth driver. Every match played = one potential impression on Instagram.

**Pillar 2: Player Features.** "Player of the Week" spotlight posts featuring a real user, their GSM stats, their club, and a quote about their experience. This validates social proof and gives people a reason to tag GSM.

**Pillar 3: Behind the Build.** Authentic content showing the app in development, courtside testing, founder story. This humanises the brand and builds early community investment in the product's success.

**Pillar 4: Padel Culture.** Repost and engage with Athens padel content. Comment on local club posts. React to professional padel news. Position GSM as part of the padel conversation, not just a utility.

**Pillar 5: League Narrative.** Weekly updates during the founding league: standings, upsets, streak milestones, group-stage drama. Treat it like a mini sports broadcast. This is the content that makes people outside the league want to join Season One.

## 5. MVP Scope: Padel-Specific Tweaks

The core MVP defined in the strategic review remains valid. The following adjustments optimise it for padel-first launch.

### 5.1 Changes to Existing MVP Scope

| Area | Original | Padel-First Adjustment |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Default sport** | No default; user picks during onboarding | Padel pre-selected. User can change, but padel is the starting point. |
| **Score input** | Tennis-style set scoring (6-4, 7-5) | Support padel scoring natively: best of 3 sets, games to 6, tiebreak at 6-6. Padel-specific labels in the UI. |
| **Discovery filters** | Sport + level + distance | Add "doubles partner wanted" filter. Padel is almost always doubles; the app should make it easy to find a 4th player. |
| **Broadcast message** | "I'm ready to play" | Add option: "Looking for a 4th" vs "Looking for opponents (pair)". Padel matchmaking often involves assembling a group, not just 1v1. |
| **Level descriptions** | Generic: beginner/intermediate/advanced/pro | Padel-contextual: "I'm learning the basics" / "I play regularly and use the glass walls" / "I compete in local tournaments" / "I train competitively" |
| **Victory share card** | Generic GSM template | Padel-branded template with padel iconography. Separate templates per sport in future. |

### 5.2 Doubles Support: The Critical Padel Feature

This is the single most important padel-specific product decision. Padel is overwhelmingly played as doubles. The current spec models matches as 1v1 (two participants). For padel MVP, the match model needs to support 2v2.

| Minimum Viable Doubles |
| :---- |
| Extend the match model to support 4 participants with team assignment (Team A / Team B). Score is logged per match, not per player. Points are awarded to each player on the winning team independently. Discovery should allow searching for "a 4th player" as a first-class use case, not an afterthought. |

This is the one area where the backend spec needs meaningful extension before padel launch. The state machine, scoring engine, and journal all work as-is; the match participant model is the only structural change.

## 6. Updated Product Vision Language

The product vision document should be updated to reflect padel-first positioning. Key changes:

1. **One-line pitch becomes:** "GSM is the first rating system for amateur padel. Find opponents, play matches, build your score. Tennis and pickleball built-in."

2. **The Problem section adds:** A paragraph about padel's specific challenges — the sport is new enough that there are no established structures for competitive amateur play. Clubs are opening weekly, but there's no connective tissue between them.

3. **The Biggest Vision becomes:** "Year 1: the padel matchmaking utility in Athens. Year 2: the competitive platform across racket sports and cities. Year 3: the global scoring standard."

4. **Why Now adds:** "Padel is the only major racket sport with no amateur scoring standard. Tennis has UTR and WTN. Padel has nothing. GSM fills the gap."
